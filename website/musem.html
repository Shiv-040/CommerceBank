Here are some of Kansas City's best museums

National WW1 Museum and Memorial

The National World War 1 Museum and Memorial in Kansas City was opened in 2004. It was created to serve as tribute to those who served in World War 1 and serves to tell the tale on one of the worlds greatests wars and America's part in it. It is a great place to hear differing stories and experience the world from a later time.

Address: 2 Memorial Dr, Kansas City, MO 64108

Cost: $10-18

Uniqueness: They were reopened multiple times and each time seems to get better and better!

Nelson-Atkins Museum of Art

The Nelson-Atkins Museum of Art in Kansas City was opened in 1933 and serves as a home to experience the worlds differing art. These various arts include late European paintings, different Asian paintings and statues and even American paintings. It is a great way to experience a different form of media and a great idea to spend a day with your kids!

Address: 4525 Oak St, Kansas City, MO 64111

Cost: FREE

Uniqueness: There are multiple types of art that anyone can enjoy!

Arabia Steamboat Museum

The Arabia Steamboat Museum in Kansas City was opened in 1991 and serves as a time capsule of life in American frontier. It shows the different steamboats that crossed through the Missouri river and helped travel the new frontier. Overall a great place to learn something new and is great for a day with the kids!

Address: 400 Grand Blvd, Kansas City, MO 64106

Cost: $5-15

Uniqueness: It is on the river market where you can explore more then just the museum

Negro Leagues Baseball Museum

The Negro Leagues Baseball Museum in Kansas City was opened in 1990 and serves to honor the Negro National League and displays the different players and officals at that time. It is a great place if you are a sports history buff and could be a great place if you are into baseball!

Address: 1616 E 18th St, Kansas City, MO 64108

Cost: $5-10

Uniqueness: It welcomes more then two million visitors each year! Who's to say you can't be one!
